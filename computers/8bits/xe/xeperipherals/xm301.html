The Atari XM301 Modem

 



        The Atari XM301 Modem was Atari's third 300 Baud modem to be produced.   The Atari XM301 was basically a smaller repackaged version of the Atari 835/1030 modems produced by Atari, Inc.    The Modem required no power, but instead received power from the SIO (Serial I/O) connection cable.   Like the Atari 410 cassette recorders the XM301 modem had no SIO daisy chain port on it, so it had to be at the end of the computers SIO chain.   The modem had 1 telco connector on it and an LED for status.    It was a simple, low cost connection device for Atari 8-bit home computers.
 

Note: Due to the lack of a 12v+ line on the Atari 1200XL line of computers, the XM301 modem would not work on them with a simple modification to the SIO port on the 1200XL to restore this voltage line.
