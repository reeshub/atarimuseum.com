Business "NOT" as usual...

 

        After the mishaps of 1982, Atari had a sobering wake up in the form of bad press, poor sales and bruised ego's.   1983 would be a year to change course and set about correcting many of its product line shortcomings.     Several projects were begun in both the Consumer Electronics group and the Home Computer group.     In the Consumer arena, engineering began to examine the possibilities of new game system designs, some were based around doing multi-TIA systems, combining the TIA and GTIA together.    Others looked to a 3rd party company that had been developing some of Atari's 2600, 5200 and home computer games called General Computer Corporation (GCC).    They were now pitching a new game system based around a new graphics processor that could work side by side with the Atari TIA chip.      Adding to this mix was a Japanese firm named Nintendo.    They approached Atari to discuss licensing and distribution of their "Famicom" console in the United States under the Atari name.    So from the consumer front, there was a lot on the plate to choose from and bring to market.

 

        Meanwhile in the Home Computer division, 1983 would bring about some changes to the current home computer line.    Newly designed replacement systems to put in place of the 1200XL were being readied - the 600XL (16K, RF only) computer and the 800XL (64K, RF and Composite video) computer.     Both sported something entirely new for any of Atari's home computers.    A new access panel was on the back of the systems - marked Expansion Bus.     This was Atari's new expansion port, a parallel bus that connected into all of the major IC's within the system.   Plans were made to have an expansion chassis designed and built called the XL Expansion System.    This box would be a card cage for all sorts of cards and peripherals not possible on the previous 400, 800 or 1200XL computers.     CPU cards, video cards, networking, hard drive controllers and more.   A whole new world of possibilities --- once the Expansion box was completed and released.
