Where there's smoke - there's Philip Morris

    Things were going quite well for Atari up until the summer of 1983.   However, Atari's parent company - Warner Communications was about to shake things up and things around Atari changed in several ways.    Ray Kassar - former Textile Executive brought in by Warner Communications to be the CEO of Atari in 1978 was ousted from Atari over his questionable stock trading during December of 1982.    While the management switch occurred, Nintendo grew impatient that their deal with Atari for its Famicom had not been completed, so Nintendo ended negotiations and went off on its own.  In 1985 Nintendo would return with its NES "Entertainment System".

    GCC (General Computer Corp) was now in full swing of developing the new "3600" video game console for Atari based around its "MARIA" graphics processor.   This new game console would be backward compatible with Atari's 2600 and ready for June 84.

    Warner decided to hire James Morgan from Philip Morris as Atari's new CEO.     After accepting the position, he took a 2 month "vacation" and then upon taking the reigns in September 1983 decided to put ALL projects on hold while he examined the company, its products, projects and capabilities.     On top of this, holiday shipments needed to be started immediately.   However, given a decision between producing Atari's computers in its new plant which would take 30 days to gear up and produce them at a lower cost or continue producing in the current factory at a slightly higher cost, James Morgan's delay cost Atari the entire 1983 holiday computer selling season, leaving Atari's main competitor - Commodore, free and clear to literally own home computer sales for 1983.

    In the end, after James Morgan completed his review of the company, its products and its future, he canceled many strategic products.     Casualties were the Atari 1400XL, 1450XLD, 1600XL PC Computer, CP/M box, Computer add-on for the Atari 2600 and several other products.
