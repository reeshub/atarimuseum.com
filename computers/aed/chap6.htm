The "Next Generation"

    No its still 1983, not 1987 and the Next Generation was a marketing slogan tag for Atari's new "XL" computer line and not a reference to Star Trek - The Next Generation...

       As the finalized 600XL and 800XL were preparing for dealer shelves, Atari engineers were still at work on two additional "XL" computers - the 1400XL and the 1450XLD.     

Now of interesting note: the Atari 1400XL was internally codenamed the Atari 1201XL as it was originally designed back in spring of 1982 as what the original Atari 1200XL computer was supposed to be.   The 1200XL (codenamed Sweet-16) had an earlier sibling and it was codenamed "Elizabeth" which was a 1200XL with a 50 pin expansion bus header on it.

   At the last minute in the summer of 1982 the design was radically changed, its enhanced 15 pin SIO ports were replaced with a standard SIO port, and the expansion bus disappeared.    So with the new system (1400XL) having a modem and voice synthesizer added, essentially it would be a step up from the 1200XL original design, so it was internally designated the Atari 1201XL

 

    The other "Next Generation" system was the Atari 1450XLD.    Essentially the very same motherboard as the 1400XL, but with power and expansion header on the inside to allow a disk drive controller daughterboard to plug into it to control up to 2 disk drives.

These new systems would be completed and ready to ship for the 1983 holiday season...  or so everyone thought.
